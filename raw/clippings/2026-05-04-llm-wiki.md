# LLM Wiki

Source record for Andrej Karpathy's gist on the [[LLM Wiki Pattern]].

## Summary

Karpathy proposes using an LLM-maintained wiki as a persistent intermediate
layer between immutable raw sources and user queries. Instead of repeatedly
retrieving raw chunks at query time, the LLM ingests each source once, extracts
key ideas, updates concept and entity pages, and maintains cross-links,
contradiction notes, summaries, and indices.

The pattern frames Obsidian as an IDE, the LLM as the programmer, and the wiki
as a codebase. Raw sources remain immutable, while the wiki is the compiled,
living representation of accumulated understanding.

## Key Takeaways

- Raw sources are the source of truth and should not be edited by the LLM.
- The wiki compounds because summaries, links, contradictions, and synthesis
  persist after each ingest.
- A schema file such as [[AGENTS]] is what makes the LLM behave like a disciplined
  maintainer instead of a generic chatbot.
- Ingest, query, and lint are the core operations of the pattern.
- `wiki/index.md` helps both humans and agents navigate at moderate scale.
- Chronological logs are useful for reconstructing what changed and when.
