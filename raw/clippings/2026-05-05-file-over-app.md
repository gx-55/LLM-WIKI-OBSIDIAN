# File over app

Source record for Steph Ango's essay on [[File Over App]].

## Summary

Ango argues that digital artifacts last only when people can control them as
files in readable, retrievable formats. Apps are temporary, cloud services can
disappear, and proprietary formats can strand the work they contain. Files,
especially plain text or other open formats, give future users a better chance
of recovering and reusing what was made.

The essay connects this philosophy to [[Obsidian]]: the app may eventually
become obsolete, but the plain text files created with it are designed to
outlast the tool.

## Key Takeaways

- The artifact matters more than the app that created it.
- Software makers should give people ownership over their data.
- Server-bound, login-gated, or proprietary storage weakens long-term access.
- Durable files matter both for posterity and for a person's future self.
- Plain text is valuable because it can remain readable across generations of
  computing environments.
